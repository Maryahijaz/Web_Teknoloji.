Some Historical Places

Petra

An ancient and historical city built by the Nabateans,
famous for its rock-cut architecture and the system of ancient water channels.
It was named the ("Pink City") due to the color of its rocks.
Petra is also one of the Seven Wonders of the World

Wadi Rum

A tourist valley located in the south of Jordan,
is also called ("the Valley of the Moon")
due to the similarity of its topography to that of the Moon.
Wadi Rum contains a group of narrow valleys,
natural arches, steep cliffs, sloping roads,
a number of caves and thousands of rock carvings.

Al Aqaba

Aqaba is the only coastal city in Jordan, Aqaba is located on the Red Sea coast.
Aqaba was called Eilat in the past due to its strategic location and proximity to copper mines



The Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is a salt lake located in the gully of the Jordan Valley.
The Dead Sea is famous for being the lowest point on Earth.
The Dead Sea is one of the medical tourism areas,
as the composition of its water is different from natural water
because it contains a high concentration of calcium and potassium.

Wadi Al Mujib

Wadi Mujib or the Mujib River is a steep valley that flows into the Dead Sea.
Archaeologists have identified Wadi Mujib as a spot inhabited since the Chalcolithic period.
The area is described as a trading spot for salt and oil,
while the villagers today depend on agriculture as a source of income.
